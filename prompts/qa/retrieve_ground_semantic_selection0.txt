#Query#: Was the Peace of Westphalia signed in Münster and Osnabrück, Germany?
#Knowledge#: The Peace of Westphalia, comprising two peace treaties signed in October 1648 in the Westphalian cities of Osnabrück and Münster, effectively ended the Thirty Years' War (1618–1648) and brought peace to the Holy Roman Empire, concluding a devastating period of European history that resulted in the loss of approximately eight million lives. These treaties involved significant participants including Holy Roman Emperor Ferdinand III, the kingdoms of France and Sweden, and their respective allies among the princes of the Holy Roman Empire. The negotiation process was notably lengthy and complex. The discussions were held in two different cities, Osnabrück and Münster, as each side preferred to negotiate on territory under its own control. Over the course of the negotiations, a total of 109 delegations representing the belligerent states arrived, though not all delegations were present simultaneously. To formally end the war within the Empire, two specific treaties were signed: the Treaty of Münster and the Treaty of Osnabrück.
#Answer#: Yes, the Peace of Westphalia was signed in Münster and Osnabrück, Germany.

#Query#: Which city hosted the 1936 Summer Olympics?

This event marked the second and most recent occasion when the International Olympic Committee convened in a city bidding to host the Games. Initially, several other cities had expressed interest in hosting the Olympics, including Alexandria, Budapest, Buenos Aires, Cologne, Dublin, Frankfurt, Helsinki, Lausanne, Montevideo, Nuremberg, Rio de Janeiro, and Rome, but they eventually withdrew from the race. Notably, Helsinki, Rome, Barcelona, and Rio de Janeiro later hosted the Olympics in 1952, 1960, 1992, and 2016, respectively.
